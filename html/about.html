Design

This quote from Alan Kay perfectly sums up my philosophy in interaction design:

"Simple things should be simple.
Complex things should be possible."

I recognize the value of minimalism in contemporary design. But I believe we must resist the temptation of uniformity. Typography, color and animations offer endless opportunities to create original experiences.

Research

I was introduced to the field of Human-Computer Interaction by Wendy Mackay and Michel Beaudoin-Lafon during an internship at INRIA, in Paris. You may have recognized the influence of Michel's paper "Designing Interaction, not Interfaces" on the title of this page.

I now work with Professor Joanna McGrenere in the Multimodal User Experience lab at UBC. My current research interest is personalization, encompassing adaptable and adaptive interfaces.
